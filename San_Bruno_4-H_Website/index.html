What do 4-H members do?

They raise prize winning livestock, grow delicious vegetables, produce and
perform dramatic productions, bake amazing cakes and cookies, help in
the community, and participate in (and win!) robotics competitions. Head,
Heart, Hands, and Health are the four Hs in 4-H, and these values are
present in all our projects and activities: Head, Heart, Hands, and
Health.

History

Since its humble beginnings more than 100 years ago, 4-H has grown to become the nation’s largest youth development organization. The 4-H idea is simple: help young people and their families gain the skills they need to be proactive forces in their communities and develop ideas for a more innovative economy. That idea was the catalyst to begin the 4-H movement, and those values continue today. As one of the first youth development organizations in America, 4-H opened the door for young people to learn leadership skills and explore ways to give back. 4-H revolutionized how youth connected to practical, hands-on learning experiences while outside of the classroom.

What has 4-H done for San Bruno?

The San Bruno 4-H club is very active about participating in community events. We can be seen at Operation Clean Sweep in May, the Posy Parade in June, and Food and Clothing drives throughout the year. We also encourage all our members to exhibit project accomplishments in the San Mateo County Fair every June and many of our projects (be they animal exhibits, or clothing or artwork) can be seen on display in the large animal barn and 4-H exhibit hall.

How does 4-H work?

The San Bruno 4-H Club has a small farm and club house in San Bruno. The club offers hands-on projects and activities, directed by adult volunteers, for youth ages 5–19. Each youth member enrolls in the projects they are interested in, plus they attend monthly general meetings, fun days, farm clean-up days, and other organized club activities. A unique aspect of 4-H is that the club is run by the youth officers and members, so members learn leadership, good citizenship, and active participation in governance.

Not just cows and cake...

While poultry, gardening, and rabbits are very popular projects, the San Bruno 4-H club has very much more to offer! 4-H has embraced the development of new technologies, and has incorporated new projects and curriculum to reflect these new areas of expertise. The San Bruno 4-H club offers the only robotics project in the entire county, as well as projects like rocketry!

See a project in another club that seems interesting? No problem! Membership to any club in the San Mateo County allows your child to participate in all projects offered by any other club in the county. So do you live in Pacifica and still want to participate in one of our projects? No problem! Become a 4-H member at your local club and come check us out!

  • 4-H Record Books

    Come learn about the 4-H Online Record Book (ORB), of which I am a big fan. We cover setting up a members’ ORB; identifying activities for the eight areas of record book credit; entering data; and printing and compiling forms in the physical record book. Bring laptops to log in to your ORB account.

  • Astronomy and Rocketry

    Look up towards the sky and learn about the stars and planets that surround you. Leave the surface of the Earth and come learn fascinating facts about planets, black holes, galaxies,and our Sun!

  • Beekeeping

    Our thriving resident bees allow monthly visits into their world under the guidance of our expert beekeeper. Come for a visit and leave with a better appreciation of what bees do for us!

  • BizWorld

    BizWorld is a County-Wide project. The curriculum geared for the 3rd through 8th grade, but is open to all members. This project will teach children the basics of entrepreneurship, business, and finance in a hands-on manner. This project gives children the opportunity to learn first-hand how to start and operate their own business. Working in teams, kids start, fund, and run their own company in the friendship bracelet industry. The teams will sell their products at the combined Favorite Foods Day/Fashion Review/Crafts Expo/SET event to be held next Spring.Note: If your child registers, it will be very important that they attend all seven meetings because of the team work aspect of this project. Make sure before registering that they can make all the meetings [emergencies and unavoidable conflicts excepted].Contact Joan Chipser (joanchipser@sbcglobal.net) for more information.

  • Cooking

    The cooking project teaches basic cooking skills through preparation of one or more recipes at each meeting.After the recipe is prepared, everyone sits down and shares the food together. Basic cooking and food safety issues are also covered.

  • Crafts

    In the Hobbycrafts Project members learn valuable skills such as basic sewing skills, fine motor skills, making do with less, reusing common household items, and basic art skills that translate into how you dress or how you decorate a room!

  • Dog Care

    In this project, members learn what it takes to keep man’s best friend happy and healthy. What foods are good and not so good, grooming, the parts of a dog, how dogs communicate, and the many roles that dogs play in our lives.

  • Gardening/Ecology

    No need to have a green thumb to join our Gardening and Ecology project, but be prepared to leave with one! As a group we learn how to plan and grow a garden in a sustainable and respectful manner. Our produce is our reward!

  • Legos

    There's no limit to what you can build with Legos with a little creativity and imagination. From basic block towers, to fantastic animals, dinosaurs, boats, cars, rocket ships, super heroes, and all at the 4-H farm!

  • Poultry

    Members learn about all types of poultry – chickens, ducks, turkeys, and more! They study how to take care of them, how to diagnose and treat illnesses, how to show a bird, attend poultry shows and field days.

  • Sewing

    Have fun and learn the basics of sewing construction. This project will focus on basic sewing construction skills and pattern and fabric selection. Participants will work on a different project each week that can be exhibited at the county fair.

  • Small market animals

    This project is going to focus on how to choose, raise and show small market animals. The ultimate goal will be for members to choose which type(s) of animals they would like to raise and then take them to fair this year to show and sell at auction. These animals include Turkeys, Chickens and Rabbits.

Start Your Own!

Interested in starting a project? Talk to us. The 4-H program can help with curriculum in many areas. You are only limited by your imagination!
